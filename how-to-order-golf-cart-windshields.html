How to Order Custom Golf Cart Windshields: MORFRIEND OEM Guide

Your Complete Walkthrough from Concept to Delivery

Updated July 2026 6 min read OEM Guide

When standard off-the-shelf windshields won't fit your fleet, brand identity, or unique vehicle design, custom OEM manufacturing is the solution. At MORFRIEND, we specialize in custom acrylic golf cart windshields built to your exact specifications —from precise dimensions to branded finishes. All our products are DOT and AMECA certified and meet FMVSS 205 safety standards. Below is a complete walkthrough of our OEM production process.

Three Ways to Customize Your Golf Cart Windshield

1. Custom Dimensions & Thickness

Every vehicle model and use case is different. We manufacture custom-sized golf cart windshields with tailored widths, heights, and thicknesses ranging from 4mm to 6mm, engineered to fit Club Car, EZGO, Yamaha, and proprietary fleet vehicles alike.

Simply provide your CAD drawings, physical templates, or vehicle model and year —our engineering team will match every detail, including precise mounting hole placement.

2. Shape & Style Options

Beyond standard rectangular panels, we produce folding (two-piece) windshields, one-piece fixed windshields, vented designs, and curved profile windshields. Edge finishing options include polished, rounded, and chamfered edges.

You can also choose from clear, lightly tinted, or smoke-tinted acrylic with built-in UV stabilization —ideal for sunny climates and branded color schemes.

3. Logo & Graphic Printing

Turn every windshield into a branding opportunity. We offer high-precision screen printing, UV-cured printing, and laser marking for company logos, fleet numbers, safety markings, or decorative patterns.

Printed graphics are durable, weather-resistant, and applied before final curing to ensure long-lasting adhesion without peeling or fading.

Our 4-Step OEM Process

1

Step 1: Consultation & Quotation

Share your specifications —dimensions, material grade, order quantity, and any artwork files. Our engineering team reviews design compatibility and provides a detailed quote within 24—8 hours, including lead time and tooling costs.

2

Step 2: Sampling & Prototyping

Before full production, we manufacture 1— sample units for fit and quality verification. This is your opportunity to test mounting alignment, optical clarity, and print accuracy on the actual vehicle.

3

Step 3: Mass Production

Once samples are approved, production begins. Our in-house facility handles cutting, thermoforming, edge polishing, drilling, and printing —ensuring consistent quality across every unit. Typical lead time: 15—5 days, depending on order volume.

4

Step 4: Quality Inspection & Shipping

Every windshield undergoes optical clarity, dimensional accuracy, and impact resistance testing before packaging. We ship worldwide with protective foam packaging to prevent scratches or cracks in transit.
